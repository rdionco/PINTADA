SPOLIARIUM

Artist: Juan Luna Year: 1884 Technique: Oil Paint

The Spoliarium is a painting by Filipino artist Juan Luna. Luna entered the picture at the Exposición Nacional de Bellas Artes in Madrid in 1884, where it won the first gold medal.

The film depicts a devastation scene at a Roman circus, as dead gladiators are stripped of their weapons and garments.

The painting reflects Luna’s deep nationalism and his yearning for freedom and justice for the Filipino people. Spoliarium became a powerful symbol, inspiring Filipinos to resist the political oppression enforced by Spanish colonial rule.
